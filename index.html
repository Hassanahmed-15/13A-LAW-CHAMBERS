02 / The Chambers

A working set, not a marketing firm.

§ 02

We are a compact set of advocates operating from chambers №13·A, taking instruction on matters where the quality of reasoning, not the weight of the file, determines the outcome.

Our work falls into three tempos — advisory, litigation, and advocacy at the Bar. We move between them carefully, and only on matters we are genuinely suited to. We would rather refer a brief to a colleague than hold it poorly.

Clients come to us through other lawyers, former clients, and — increasingly — from founders and families who have grown tired of large firms where partners are introduced and then never seen again.

08 / Questions

Questions clients actually ask.

§ 08
Do you take matters outside Lahore? +
Yes. We regularly appear in Islamabad, Karachi, and appellate fora. For foreign-seated arbitration we work alongside local counsel of your choosing.
Is the first consultation chargeable? +
A 30-minute intake call is complimentary. A substantive first opinion is billed at an agreed flat fee, disclosed before the meeting.
How do you structure your fees? +
Wherever possible, fixed-fee per stage. Hourly billing only on matters whose scope genuinely cannot be scoped — and always against a written cap.
How is confidentiality handled? +
All instructions are privileged. Our systems are self-hosted and encrypted. We never publish a matter in a form that could identify a client.
Can you act against a large institution? +
Frequently. We maintain no standing retainers with commercial banks or large corporates — specifically to preserve the ability to act for individuals against them.
Who will actually handle my matter? +
Named in writing before work begins. The counsel you meet at intake is the counsel on the brief — no silent handoffs to juniors.
