I  ·  About

A chambers built on independence and shared standards.

13-A Law Chambers is a shared chambers in Islamabad, bringing together advocates of the Supreme Court of Pakistan who choose to practise side by side under a common roof.

Each member maintains their own clients, their own files, and their own professional standards — supported by a collegiate environment that encourages exchange, scrutiny, and a shared commitment to the rule of law.

Where a matter benefits from collaboration, members may, with the client's informed consent, work jointly on a brief. Otherwise, every advocate practises in their own right and on their own responsibility.

Important Notice

13-A Law Chambers is a shared chambers arrangement. Each lawyer practises independently and maintains their own client relationships and professional responsibilities. We are not a partnership or law firm, and no lawyer here is liable for the work or conduct of any other.

Where appropriate and with the informed consent of the client, one or more advocates may be engaged or may collaborate on a particular matter. Any such engagement is matter-specific and does not create a partnership, joint venture, or any continuing professional association between the advocates involved.

Nothing on this website shall be construed as indicating the existence of a partnership, joint venture, or any other form of professional association between the occupants of these chambers.

III  ·  Practice

Areas of work.

Members of chambers accept instructions across a broad range of contentious and advisory matters before the District Courts, High Courts, Supreme Court, special tribunals and regulatory bodies of Pakistan.

i.
Civil & Commercial

Contractual disputes, property and rent matters, recovery suits, banking and corporate litigation before the civil and commercial courts.

ii.
Constitutional

Writ petitions, judicial review, fundamental rights and public-interest litigation in the High Courts and Supreme Court of Pakistan.

iii.
Criminal & White-Collar

Trial, bail and appellate work across the criminal jurisdiction, including economic offences, accountability and high-profile prosecutions.

iv.
Regulatory & Telecoms

Telecommunications, media, competition, public procurement and energy regulation before sectoral regulators and tribunals.

v.
Arbitration & ADR

Domestic and international arbitration, mediation and enforcement of awards, including FIDIC and commercial disputes.

vi.
Family & Immigration

Matrimonial, custody, maintenance and inheritance matters, alongside immigration and citizenship advice and representation.
